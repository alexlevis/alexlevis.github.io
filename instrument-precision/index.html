A note on instruments and precision variables

Alex Levis
 2024-06-11  92 words   One minute 
Contents
  • Graphs, Instruments, and Precision Variables

Recently, I was listening to the Casual Inference podcast (Season 3 Episode 10) by Lucy D’Agostino McGowan and Ellie Murrary, and a question was raised: does including an instrument in, e.g., a propensity score model, cause bias? I believe the answer to this question, in a general sense, is no. However, such a choice will impact the efficiency of our estimates. In this post, I want to review a pair of lemmas from an excellent paper by Rotnitzky & Smucler (2020; JMLR) that formally addresses this issue.
